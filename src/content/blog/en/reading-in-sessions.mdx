Most reading apps want your attention forever. Open the app, scroll the feed, feel guilty about your streak, repeat.

Readen is built on a different unit: the session.

## What a session is

A session is a bounded moment of reading. You open a book, start the timer, slide to your current page, and read until you are done for now. When you close the session, Readen shows you a literary quote and logs the time.

No infinite scroll. No social pressure. Just the book and the clock.

## Streaks without guilt

We do track weekly streaks (dots for days you read), but they are quiet. A gentle record, not a punishment. Miss a day and your library is still there, unchanged, waiting.

## Why this works

Finishing books is not about willpower. It is about rhythm. Short, regular sessions beat rare marathons. The page slider means you always know where you left off. The timer means you know how much life you are putting into reading.

That is the philosophy: calm tools for a calm habit.
