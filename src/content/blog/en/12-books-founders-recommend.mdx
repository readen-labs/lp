The best book recommendations do not come from algorithms. They come from people who have built something hard and read their way through the problem.

Readen's discover catalog includes 4,070 books recommended by 572 world-class readers. Here are twelve that appear again and again on founders' shelves.

## The shelf

- **Sapiens**, Yuval Noah Harari. The macro lens every builder needs.
- **The Hard Thing About Hard Things**, Ben Horowitz. Honest about the cost of building.
- **Zero to One**, Peter Thiel. Contrarian thinking as a discipline.
- **Thinking, Fast and Slow**, Daniel Kahneman. How decisions actually get made.
- **The Lean Startup**, Eric Ries. Still the vocabulary of iteration.
- **Poor Charlie's Almanack**, Charlie Munger. Mental models as a operating system.
- **Shoe Dog**, Phil Knight. Building Nike, told without myth-making.
- **The Innovator's Dilemma**, Clayton Christensen. Why incumbents fail.
- **High Output Management**, Andy Grove. Operations as craft.
- **The Design of Everyday Things**, Don Norman. Why products feel wrong.
- **Atomic Habits**, James Clear. Systems over goals.
- **Man's Search for Meaning**, Viktor Frankl. Perspective when it gets hard.

## Why this matters

These are not a reading list to finish. They are a starting shelf: the books that keep showing up when you ask the people who built the future what they read along the way.
