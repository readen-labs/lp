Every book app shows covers the same way: a flat rectangle with rounded corners. It works. It is also wrong.

Books are not cards. A hardcover has a spine, a ridge where the cloth meets the board, a subtle grain that catches light differently on cream versus black. When you strip that away, a library feels like a spreadsheet.

## The goodbooks starting point

We studied goodbooks.io, the catalog that inspired Readen's discover section. Their cover treatment uses a multiply texture: a PNG overlaid on the art that darkens toward the spine. On white covers it reads as shadow. On black covers it disappears.

We wanted the same relief on every cover color.

## Ambient gray 80

The breakthrough was converting the multiply texture to an ambient-gray compositing model. Instead of darkening the art underneath, the texture asset composites toward a fixed gray (80 on the 0–255 scale). On light covers, the relief reads dark, identical to goodbooks. On black covers, the same relief reveals itself as soft lighter gray.

Plain alpha compositing. No blend modes. Works everywhere.

## Near-square corners

One more thing: book covers are not cards. Our border radius scales with width but caps at 4px. At hero sizes, covers are nearly square-cornered. The texture does the rest.
